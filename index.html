Werewolf Trials in Early Modern France

A lesser known subset of the witch hunts, werewolf trials saw men, women and even children accused of performing violent acts in the forms of wolves, an ability gained through a diabolic master. These trials share common underlying political and religious influences, but they also varied widely across space and over time. This site will, once I'm done building it, allow you to explore some of the around 300 estimated cases by clicking through an interactive map. There will also be pages going more deeply into the political, social and religious background, and the way the trials changed over time. I've linked to original sources where they are available online, and to secondary sources where academics have accessed trial records only available in physical archives. The contents of this website are based on the original research I completed for my MA dissertation, "The Werewolf Institutionalised: Class, Madness, and Leniency in Early Modern French Werewolf Trials".
